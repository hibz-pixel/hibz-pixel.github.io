Different Worlds in Modesty

Read Time: 5 minutes

Modest fashion has found its place in haute couture, with designers and models reshaping the narrative around modesty on the runway. Models like Rawdah Mohamed, who wears the hijab with pride, have redefined beauty standards and shown that covering up can be as expressive as revealing. Fashion houses like Iris van Herpen and Schiaparelli also exemplify this by creating stunning garments that prioritize structure and form over exposed skin. Their designs focus on shapes, textures, and intricate details rather than showing off the body. These haute couture pieces, though avant-garde, align with the principles of modesty as they celebrate the art of fashion without the need for revealing silhouettes.

Streetwear, known for its laid-back, oversized aesthetic, naturally lends itself to modest fashion. Often characterized by baggy pants, oversized hoodies, and loose-fitting tops, streetwear provides a sense of comfort while maintaining a modest appearance. Its ability to blend casual wear with modesty makes it accessible and versatile for people of all backgrounds. Modest fashion enthusiasts can layer or style streetwear pieces in ways that not only align with their personal values but also stay on-trend. With its origins rooted in counter-culture, streetwear reflects a form of rebellion that can coincide with modesty by choosing comfort and coverage over form-fitting designs.

Islamic modesty is often considered the ultimate form of covering in the fashion world. Adhering to religious guidelines, it typically involves wearing loose, non-revealing clothing that only shows the hands and face, while the hair is covered with a hijab or headscarf. This form of modesty emphasizes dignity and humility, creating a balance between personal expression and religious observance. While the clothing is usually baggy and covers the entire body, Islamic modest fashion can still be incredibly stylish, with designers catering to this demographic by offering elegant abayas, flowy maxi dresses, and long-sleeve tops that adhere to Islamic principles while reflecting modern fashion trends.

These different forms of modesty—from haute couture to streetwear to Islamic fashion—showcase how diverse modest fashion can be while still fitting seamlessly into the world of style. Whether it's avant-garde runway designs, casual streetwear, or religiously inspired attire, modesty in fashion is about more than just covering up; it's about expressing oneself in ways that align with personal or cultural values. This diversity within modest fashion highlights that there is no single way to dress modestly, and the fashion world is becoming increasingly inclusive of these different expressions.
